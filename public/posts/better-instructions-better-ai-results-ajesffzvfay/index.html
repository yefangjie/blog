Better Instructions, Better AI Results: The Communication Gap

Introduction

This IBM Technology video explores a fundamental shift in how we interact with AI systems - the recognition that clear, precise communication with AI agents requires the same (or greater) level of care as managing human teams.

The Management Analogy

The speaker draws an interesting parallel between managing AI agents and raising children:

“It’s the argument like are you becoming a better manager when you have children, right? And I think you do because you have more patience.”

This unexpected comparison highlights how AI agents, like children, require:

  • Clear guidance
  • Patience with limitations
  • Understanding of context
  • Explicit rather than implicit communication

The Communication Problem

Throwing Things Over the Fence

The video identifies a common anti-pattern in human communication:

“We throw something over the fence. We are not describing what we really want. There’s a lot of mind reading going on and the system just goes off the rails.”

This describes how humans often:

  • Assume shared context
  • Expect others to fill gaps
  • Rely on implicit understanding
  • Fail to specify requirements completely

AI as a Mirror

AI systems are less forgiving than human collaborators because:

  • They don’t have “the meeting after the meeting”
  • They can’t ask peers for clarification
  • They execute exactly what was requested, not what was intended
  • The outcome directly reflects the quality of instructions

The Gap-Filling Problem

With human teams, communication gaps are naturally filled through:

  • Follow-up questions
  • Peer discussions
  • Informal clarifications
  • Cultural context sharing

AI agents lack these social mechanisms, making the initial instructions the only source of truth.

Improving AI Communication

Key Principles

  1. Be Explicit: State requirements completely rather than assuming context
  2. Be Precise: Use specific language rather than vague descriptions
  3. Provide Examples: Show what success looks like
  4. Define Constraints: Set clear boundaries and limitations
  5. Iterate: Use feedback loops to refine instructions

The Learning Opportunity

Working with AI agents provides immediate, unfiltered feedback on communication quality:

“AI is less forgiving because there’s no other place to go and so you just see the outcome of your poor instructions.”

This creates a unique opportunity to improve communication skills that transfer to human interactions as well.

Conclusion

As AI agents become more capable, the bottleneck shifts from technical implementation to communication quality. Those who learn to provide clear, comprehensive instructions will extract significantly more value from AI systems than those who treat them as mind readers.

The video suggests that effective AI interaction is ultimately a management skill - requiring patience, clarity, and the ability to translate intent into actionable direction.
