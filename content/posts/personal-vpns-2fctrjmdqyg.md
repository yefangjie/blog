## Overview

This IBM Security Intelligence episode demystifies personal VPNs, separating marketing hype from technical reality.

## What VPNs Actually Do

### Core Functions
- **Traffic encryption** between your device and VPN server
- **IP address masking** by routing through VPN server
- **Location spoofing** to bypass geographic restrictions
- **Network tunneling** on untrusted networks

### What VPNs Don't Do
- Complete anonymity (fingerprinting still works)
- Protection from all tracking
- Automatic security (user behavior matters)
- Malware protection

## Common Encryption Myths

### Myth 1: "VPNs Make You Completely Anonymous"
**Reality**: VPNs hide your IP but don't prevent browser fingerprinting, cookie tracking, or account-based tracking.

### Myth 2: "Military-Grade Encryption Means Unbreakable"
**Reality**: Most VPNs use standard AES-256. The "military-grade" label is marketing.

### Myth 3: "Free VPNs Are Just as Good"
**Reality**: Free VPNs often sell user data and have weaker security practices.
